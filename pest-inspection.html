In general, leakage and water play a major role in promoting and supporting the activity of wood insects.

The termite at the beginning of the building is examined for any signs of mud or damage.

Leaks and ponding water are noted, as these are a source of moisture that would support termite activity and promote wood decay.

The hot water unit relief valve and air conditioning unit condensate pipe provide a constant source of water and can support a large whale colony.

Garden beds and irrigation are also a good source of moisture and should never be constructed at the base of the building.

The subfloor area is a critical area to check. Humidity and poorly ventilated areas provide ideal conditions for woodpeckers.

FAQ

What is a pest inspection?
For a wood pest inspection or termite inspection a professional pest inspector performs a visual pest and termite inspection of all accessible and available areas of a property for the presence of active termites and / or whale damage. The results of the pest inspection include conditions suitable for termite infestation, risks and recommendations for termite management programs.

How much is a pest inspection?
The cost of pest inspection may vary depending on the size of the property and the level of inspection you need. Pre-purchase pest inspections cost around $ 150- $ 300. Combined building and pest inspection costs around $ 500- $ 550. See Our Price

How long does a pest inspection take?
On average, pest inspections take about 60 minutes for a thorough inspection, depending on the size and condition of the home and property (e.g. chaotic; storage of personal items, etc.).

Why Buy a Pre-Purchase Pest Inspection?
To buy a home, you need a pre purchase pest and wood inspection (Termite Inspection) as well as a pre purchase building inspection.
